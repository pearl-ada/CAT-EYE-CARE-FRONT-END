CAT-EYE CARE

EXECRISES

After cataract surgery, light exercises like walking, gentle stretching, cycling, running, golf, and tennis can be done within a week. Moderate activities like brisk walking, jogging, yoga, and dancing are suitable after 2 weeks, while more intense exercises such as weightlifting, running, swimming, and intense cycling can be resumed around 4 to 6 weeks post-surgery.

Some eye exercises include;

Palming Technique

1. Rub your palms together to generate heat.

2. Close your eyes and place your warm palms over them without applying pressure.

3. Relax and breathe deeply for a few minutes.

Eye Circles

1. Sit comfortably and look straight ahead.

>2. Slowly move your eyes in a circular motion, first clockwise and then counterclockwise.

3. Repeat this exercise several times.

Near and Far Focus

1. Hold a pen or finger at arm's length and focus on it.

2. Slowly bring the object closer to your eyes while maintaining focus.

3. Move the object back to arm's length and repeat the process.

Blinking

1. Sit comfortably and blink your eyes rapidly for 30 seconds.

2. Close your eyes and relax for a few seconds.

3. Repeat this exercise a few times to help lubricate your eyes and reduce strain.

Pencil Push-Up

1. Hold a pencil or pen at arm's length, with the tip pointing downward.

2. Focus on the tip of the pencil.

3. Slowly bring the pencil closer to your nose while maintaining focus on the tip.

4. When you reach the point where your eyes can no longer focus on the tip, move the pencil away from your nose and repeat the process.

Brock String Exercise

1. Obtain a Brock string (a string with colored beads at regular intervals).

2. Hold the string at arm's length, focusing on the bead closest to your face.

3. Slowly move the string closer to your face while maintaining focus on the bead.

4. Once the bead is at a comfortable distance, switch your focus to the next bead and repeat the process.

Eye Massage

1. Gently massage your temples and the area around your eyes with your fingertips.

2. Apply light pressure and move your fingers in small circular motions.

3. This can help reduce tension and improve blood flow to the eyes.

Visual Scanning

1. Look at a detailed picture or object from a distance.

2. Slowly scan the picture or object with your eyes, focusing on different parts.

3. This exercise can help improve your eye's ability to move and focus on different areas.
